# ChainIQ Product Roadmap

> Strategic product development plan organized by Now/Next/Later framework, aligned with the 6-layer architecture and market positioning as the first Arabic-native AI content intelligence platform.

---

## Roadmap Philosophy

ChainIQ follows a **phased release strategy** driven by three principles:

1. **Customer-validated priorities** — Roadmap decisions are informed by the feedback pipeline (RICE-scored), not assumptions
2. **Revenue-enabling first** — Features that directly impact paying customers' ROI ship before nice-to-haves
3. **Arabic excellence before language breadth** — Perfect Arabic content quality before expanding to additional languages

## Public Roadmap Visibility

### Decision: Semi-Public Roadmap

**Approach:** Publish a simplified version of this roadmap on the ChainIQ website/dashboard.

**What to show publicly:**
- NOW items with approximate timelines (month-level, not day-level)
- NEXT items with quarter-level timelines
- LATER items as "Exploring" without dates
- Completed items with "Shipped" badges and changelog links

**What to keep internal:**
- Specific technical implementation details
- RICE scores and prioritization rationale
- Revenue targets and business metrics
- Competitive positioning strategy

**Why semi-public:**
- Builds trust with customers — they see the platform is actively developing
- Reduces "will you ever build X?" support questions
- Creates anticipation for upcoming features (retention driver)
- Signals to competitors that we are investing deeply in Arabic content intelligence
- Allows prospects to evaluate long-term fit before purchasing

**Update cadence:** Monthly review and update of public roadmap, aligned with changelog releases.

## Roadmap Governance

### Change Process
1. New roadmap items must come through the feedback pipeline with RICE scoring
2. Phase-level changes (moving items between NOW/NEXT/LATER) require a monthly review
3. Emergency additions (e.g., critical security feature) can bypass RICE with documented justification
4. Items removed from the roadmap are logged with rationale in decision records

### Success Criteria Per Phase
Each phase has defined "done" criteria before the next phase begins:
- All core features shipped and stable (< 0.1% error rate)
- User documentation complete
- At least 5 users actively using the new features
- Performance benchmarks met (response time, throughput)
- Feedback collected from early adopters, critical issues addressed
