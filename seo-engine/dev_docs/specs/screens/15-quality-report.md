# Screen 15: Article Quality Report

> **Route:** `/articles/[id]/quality`
> **Service:** Quality Gate (Layer 4)
> **Type:** Report + Action
> **Complexity:** XL (8-16h)
> **Priority:** P0
> **Real-time:** No (polling during active scoring)
> **Last Updated:** 2026-03-28

---

## 1. Purpose

The Article Quality Report is the "content quality certificate" — a comprehensive, exportable breakdown proving an article meets measurable quality standards across 60 deterministic checks and 7 AI-evaluated signals. It is the primary deliverable that ships alongside every generated article, serving as auditable proof of quality for clients, internal QA for content operators, and actionable improvement instructions for the revision loop. The report surfaces an overall score (0-10), decomposes it into 7 weighted signals, expands into a 60-point SEO checklist, provides prioritized fix suggestions, and tracks score progression across revision passes.

**Success metric:** Average time from report load to revision decision < 30 seconds. 95% of articles reach a passing score within 2 revision passes. Clients reference the quality report in at least 60% of content approval conversations.

## 5. States

### 5.1 Loading State
Skeleton layout matching the full page structure. The score gauge area shows a pulsing circular skeleton. Seven horizontal bar skeletons pulse in the signal section. The checklist shows 10 category-height rectangular skeletons. No spinners — the spatial layout is preserved to prevent CLS. Duration: typically <800ms (single API call to `GET /api/quality/:articleId`).

### 5.2 Scoring In-Progress State
The article has been generated but the Quality Gate pipeline is still running. The score gauge shows an animated "Scoring..." label with a spinning indicator inside the ring. Signal bars are grayed out with "Evaluating..." placeholders. The checklist shows items ticking from gray to green/red in real-time as each check completes (optimistic streaming). A progress indicator reads "Analyzing quality... 34/60 checks complete." Polling every 3 seconds until scoring finishes. Action buttons are disabled except "Cancel Scoring."

### 5.3 Scored — Passed State
The overall score ring fills with a green gradient (score >= 7.0). The PassFailBadge shows "PASSED" in green. All 7 signal bars are visible with their individual scores. The checklist is fully populated. Suggestions may still exist (e.g., "4 optional improvements"). The "Request Revision" button is hidden or shown as secondary with "Improve Further" label. This is the happy path — the operator scans the score, sees green, and moves on.

### 5.4 Scored — Failed State
The score ring fills with a red/amber gradient (score < 7.0). The PassFailBadge shows "FAILED" in red. Failed signal bars are highlighted with a red tint. The suggestions section is emphasized with a yellow border — these are now mandatory fixes, not optional. The "Request Revision" button is primary and prominent: "Request Auto-Fix (Revision 1)." A banner above the fold reads: "This article did not meet the quality threshold. Review the suggestions below and request a revision."

### 5.5 Revision In-Progress State
After requesting a revision, the page shows a progress banner: "Revision 1 in progress — the Draft Writer is applying fixes..." The current scores remain visible (grayed slightly) as a baseline. A live log of fix actions may scroll below the banner (e.g., "Fixing: Added FAQ schema markup", "Fixing: Expanded section 4 vocabulary"). The "Request Revision" button is replaced with "Revision in progress..." (disabled). Polling every 5 seconds for completion.

### 5.6 Revision Comparison State
After a revision completes, the page reloads with the new scores. A comparison banner appears at the top: "Score improved from 6.1 to 8.4 (+2.3)." The revision history card shows both entries. The "Compare" button is enabled. When clicked, a side-by-side modal shows before/after signal bars and checklist items that flipped from fail to pass (highlighted in green).

### 5.7 Revision Loop Exhausted State
The article has gone through 2 revision passes and still fails (score < 7.0). The "Request Revision" button is disabled with tooltip: "Maximum 2 revision passes reached." A banner reads: "Auto-revision could not bring this article to passing quality. Manual editing is required." Suggestions remain visible. A "Re-Score After Manual Edit" button appears, pointing operators to the article editor.

### 5.8 Re-Scoring State
After clicking "Re-Score," the current scores fade to 50% opacity. An overlay on the score gauge shows "Re-scoring..." with a spinner. The previous scores remain visible as reference. When complete, the new scores animate in. If scores changed, a delta indicator appears briefly ("+0.4" or "-0.2"). Confirmation dialog before re-score reads: "Re-score this article? This will replace the current quality scores with fresh evaluations."

### 5.9 Empty State — Not Yet Scored
The article exists but has never been through the Quality Gate (e.g., imported content or pipeline interrupted). The score gauge shows a dashed outline with "Not Scored" text. All signal bars are empty/gray. The checklist shows "No checklist data available." A single primary CTA: "Run Quality Assessment" triggers scoring for the first time. If the article is still in the generation pipeline, a message reads: "Quality scoring will begin automatically when content generation completes."

### 5.10 Standalone Scoring State
When scoring arbitrary HTML (not a ChainIQ article), the page header changes to "Standalone Quality Report" and the breadcrumb omits the article reference. The revision history section is hidden (no revision loop for external content). The "Request Revision" button is hidden. A file upload or URL input appears at the top for submitting new content to score. The report otherwise functions identically.

### 5.11 Arabic Content State
When the article language is Arabic, the page remains in the user's UI language (LTR or RTL based on locale), but quality norms adjust visibly. The Readability signal bar shows a label: "Arabic norms applied." Readability thresholds are different (Flesch-Kincaid not applicable — uses Arabic Readability Index instead). The checklist items related to word count and vocabulary richness use Arabic morphological analysis baselines. A small info icon on the Readability bar explains: "Readability scored using Arabic morphological analysis — thresholds differ from English content."

### 5.12 PDF Export State
When "Export PDF" is clicked, a loading overlay appears: "Generating PDF report..." (1-3 seconds). The PDF is generated client-side using the rendered report data (no server round-trip for PDF generation). On completion, the browser's download dialog opens. The PDF includes: header with article title and date, overall score gauge (rendered as static SVG), 7 signal bars, full 60-point checklist, suggestions, and revision history. Styled with the ChainIQ brand, suitable for client presentation.

### 5.13 Error State — Scoring Failed
The Quality Gate engine encountered an error (e.g., AI model timeout, malformed content). The score gauge shows a red exclamation icon. A banner reads: "Quality scoring encountered an error. This does not reflect content quality — it's a system issue." Details of the error are shown in a collapsible section. A "Retry Scoring" button is prominent. If the error persists after 2 retries, a "Contact Support" link appears.

### 5.14 Partial Scores State
Some signals scored successfully but others timed out (e.g., AI Detection model was slow). The scored signals show their values normally. The failed signals show a yellow "Unavailable" badge with the specific reason. The overall score is calculated from available signals only, with a disclaimer: "Overall score based on 5/7 signals — 2 signals could not be evaluated." A "Retry Failed Signals" button appears.

### 5.15 Permission Denied State
Non-authorized users (e.g., a client viewer without quality access) see the overall score and pass/fail badge but all detail sections are collapsed and locked with a "Upgrade to view full report" message. The PDF export generates a summary-only version. Action buttons (Re-Score, Request Revision) are hidden entirely.

**Total states: 15** (exceeds 12 minimum, all 4 mandatory states explicitly described).

## 7. Data Requirements

### API Endpoints Used

| Endpoint | Method | Purpose | Cache |
|----------|--------|---------|-------|
| `/api/quality/:articleId` | GET | Full quality report (scores, checklist, signals, suggestions, history) | 5min SWR, revalidate on focus |
| `/api/quality/:articleId/checklist` | GET | 60-point checklist only (lighter payload for partial refresh) | 5min SWR |
| `/api/quality/:articleId/rescore` | POST | Trigger manual re-scoring | N/A |
| `/api/quality/:articleId/history` | GET | Revision history with score progression | 5min SWR |
| `/api/quality/:articleId/revise` | POST | Request auto-revision (sends fix instructions to Draft Writer) | N/A |
| `/api/articles/:id` | GET | Article metadata (title, language, persona) for header context | 10min SWR |

### Data Refresh Strategy

- **On mount:** Single `GET /api/quality/:articleId` fetches the complete report payload
- **Polling (scoring in-progress):** Every 3 seconds during active scoring, call lightweight status endpoint. Stop polling when status is `scored` or `error`
- **Polling (revision in-progress):** Every 5 seconds during active revision. Stop when new revision appears in history
- **After mutation (re-score, revision request):** Invalidate SWR cache, refetch full report
- **PDF export:** Uses in-memory data, no additional API call

### Data Shape (Frontend)

```typescript
interface QualityReport {
  articleId: string;
  articleTitle: string;
  articleLanguage: 'en' | 'ar' | string;
  overallScore: number;              // 0-10, one decimal
  passed: boolean;                   // true if overallScore >= threshold
  passingThreshold: number;          // typically 7.0
  revisionNumber: number;            // 0, 1, or 2
  maxRevisions: number;              // 2
  scoredAt: string;                  // ISO timestamp
  status: 'pending' | 'scoring' | 'scored' | 'error';
  scoringProgress: number | null;    // 0-60 during scoring, null when complete
  signals: SignalScores;
  eeatDimensions: EEATDimensions;
  checklist: ChecklistResults;
  suggestions: Suggestion[];
  revisionHistory: RevisionEntry[];
  error: string | null;
}

interface SignalScores {
  eeat: SignalScore;
  completeness: SignalScore;
  voiceMatch: SignalScore;
  aiDetection: SignalScore;
  freshness: SignalScore;
  techSeo: SignalScore;
  readability: SignalScore;
}

interface SignalScore {
  raw: number;                       // 0-10
  weight: number;                    // 0-1 (e.g., 0.15 for 15%)
  weighted: number;                  // raw * weight
  status: 'pass' | 'warn' | 'fail' | 'unavailable';
  adjustedForLanguage: boolean;      // true if Arabic norms applied
}

interface EEATDimensions {
  experience: number;
  expertise: number;
  authoritativeness: number;
  trustworthiness: number;
  authorCredentials: number;
  sourceCitations: number;
  technicalDepth: number;
  factualAccuracy: number;
  uniqueInsights: number;
  transparencyDisclosure: number;
}

interface ChecklistResults {
  totalChecks: number;               // 60
  passedChecks: number;
  categories: ChecklistCategory[];
}

interface ChecklistCategory {
  id: string;
  name: string;                      // e.g., "Heading Hierarchy"
  items: ChecklistItem[];
  passedCount: number;
  totalCount: number;
}

interface ChecklistItem {
  id: string;
  description: string;              // e.g., "Title tag present (<=60 chars)"
  passed: boolean;
  actualValue: string | null;       // e.g., "67 chars" when failing
  expectedValue: string | null;     // e.g., "<=60 chars"
  signal: keyof SignalScores;       // which signal this maps to
  severity: 'critical' | 'major' | 'minor';
  suggestionId: string | null;      // links to corresponding suggestion
}

interface Suggestion {
  id: string;
  priority: 'high' | 'medium' | 'low';
  text: string;
  signal: keyof SignalScores;
  estimatedImpact: number;          // score points (e.g., 0.3)
  checklistItemIds: string[];       // which checks this fixes
  autoFixable: boolean;             // can Draft Writer handle this?
}

interface RevisionEntry {
  revisionNumber: number;
  overallScore: number;
  passed: boolean;
  scoredAt: string;
  signalScores: SignalScores;
  delta: number | null;             // score change from previous revision
  fixesApplied: string[];           // descriptions of what changed
}
```

## 8. Validation Rules

| Field | Rule | Error Message |
|-------|------|---------------|
| Article ID (route param) | Valid UUID format | "Invalid article ID" (404 page) |
| Re-score action | Article must have status `scored` or `error` (not `scoring` or `pending`) | "Cannot re-score while scoring is in progress" |
| Request revision | `revisionNumber < maxRevisions` (i.e., < 2) | "Maximum revision passes (2) have been reached" |
| Request revision | Article must have status `scored` with `passed === false` or operator override | "This article already passes quality. Are you sure?" (confirmation) |
| Revision scope selector | At least one signal selected if using "selected signals only" mode | "Select at least one signal to target for revision" |
| Additional instructions textarea | Max 500 characters, no required minimum | "Instructions must be 500 characters or fewer" |
| Standalone HTML input | Non-empty, valid HTML, max 200KB | "Content must be valid HTML and under 200KB" |

**Server-side validation (defense in depth):**
- Article ownership verified against authenticated user's workspace
- Revision count enforced server-side regardless of client state
- Re-score rate limited to 1 per article per 5 minutes
- Standalone scoring rate limited to 10 per workspace per hour

## 11. Performance Requirements

| Metric | Target | Measurement |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Initial load (full report) | <800ms | Time from navigation to all sections rendered |
| Score gauge animation | 60fps | SVG ring fill animation must not drop frames |
| Checklist expand/collapse | <100ms | Time from click to animation complete |
| PDF generation | <3s | Time from click to download dialog |
| Re-score polling overhead | <500B per poll | Payload size of status check during re-scoring |
| Comparison modal open | <300ms | Time from click to modal fully rendered with diff |
| Signal bar render (7 bars) | <50ms | Time to render all 7 horizontal progress bars |
| Sticky header appear/hide | 0ms perceived | CSS-only, no JavaScript reflow |

**Optimization strategy:**
- Full report fetched in a single API call (no waterfall of sub-requests)
- Checklist categories use virtualized rendering if > 60 items visible simultaneously (accordion mitigates this)
- PDF generation uses pre-rendered SVG for the score gauge (no canvas conversion)
- Score ring animation uses CSS `stroke-dashoffset` transition (GPU-accelerated, no JS animation loop)
- E-E-A-T deep dive card content lazy-rendered on first expand (10 bars not in initial DOM)
- Comparison modal data derived from in-memory revision history (no additional API call)
- SWR with 5-minute stale-while-revalidate window prevents redundant fetches on tab switch

## 12. Accessibility

### Keyboard Navigation
- `Tab` cycles through focusable elements in logical order: score gauge region -> Re-Score button -> Export PDF button -> Request Revision button -> signal bars (each individually focusable) -> weight toggle -> E-E-A-T expand -> checklist categories (each header focusable) -> Expand All -> suggestion items -> revision history rows -> Compare button
- `Enter` or `Space` on a checklist category header expands/collapses it
- `Enter` or `Space` on "Expand All" toggles all categories
- `Escape` closes any open modal (comparison, re-score confirm, revision request) and returns focus to the trigger button
- `Arrow Up` / `Arrow Down` within the checklist navigates between items within an expanded category
- `Home` / `End` within the checklist jumps to first/last item in the expanded category

### Screen Reader Support
- Overall score gauge has `role="meter"` with `aria-valuenow="8.4"`, `aria-valuemin="0"`, `aria-valuemax="10"`, and `aria-label="Overall quality score: 8.4 out of 10, Passed"`
- Each signal bar uses `role="meter"` with `aria-valuenow`, `aria-valuemin="0"`, `aria-valuemax="10"`, and `aria-label` including signal name, score, and weight (e.g., "E-E-A-T score: 8.2 out of 10, weight 15 percent")
- Pass/fail badge uses `aria-label="Quality status: Passed"` (not just color)
- Checklist categories use `role="region"` with `aria-expanded` state on the header button
- Individual checklist items use `role="listitem"` with `aria-label` including pass/fail status and description
- Suggestions use an ordered list (`role="list"`) with priority announced: "Priority high: Remove noindex meta tag"
- Revision history entries use `role="listitem"` with full context: "Revision 0, original, score 6.1, failed, March 27"
- Comparison modal uses `role="dialog"` with `aria-labelledby` and `aria-describedby`
- Re-score and revision dialogs use `role="alertdialog"` with focus trap
- Score changes announced via `aria-live="polite"` region when re-scoring completes
- Progress during scoring uses `role="progressbar"` with `aria-valuenow` updating as checks complete

### Visual Requirements
- Score gauge colors meet WCAG 2.1 AA contrast on dark background: green (#22c55e) for pass, red (#ef4444) for fail, amber (#f59e0b) for warning
- All color-coded elements have redundant iconography: checkmark icon for pass, X icon for fail, warning triangle for issues, clock for pending
- Signal bar colors are not the sole differentiator — each bar also shows a numeric score and pass/warn/fail text label
- Suggestion priority badges use both color and text: "HIGH" (red), "MED" (amber), "LOW" (blue)
- Focus indicators: 2px ring with 3:1 minimum contrast ratio against the dark background
- `prefers-reduced-motion`: disable score ring fill animation (show final state immediately), disable smooth-scroll behaviors, disable pulse highlights on suggestion cross-references
- `prefers-contrast: more`: increase bar border visibility, add explicit borders to all card sections

### RTL Support
- Full page layout mirrors for RTL locales: breadcrumb reads right-to-left, action buttons align to the left
- Signal bars fill from right-to-left (progress direction flips)
- Checklist expand chevrons flip to point left (collapsed) / down (expanded)
- Score gauge remains centered (circular, inherently direction-neutral)
- Numeric scores remain LTR (numbers are always LTR in Arabic)
- Checklist item text renders in the article's content language (Arabic) while UI labels remain in the user's locale
- Suggestion text supports bidirectional content — mixed Arabic descriptions with English technical terms
- PDF export respects the content language direction for checklist items and suggestions
- E-E-A-T dimension labels are translated; progress bars fill direction matches locale

## 13. Edge Cases

| # | Edge Case | Handling |
|---|-----------|----------|
| 1 | Article not yet scored (pipeline still running) | Show Scoring In-Progress state with live progress. If pipeline stalled >10 minutes, show "Scoring may be stuck" warning with manual retry button |
| 2 | All 60 checklist items pass | Checklist summary shows "60/60 passed" with a celebratory green state. Categories default to collapsed (no issues to inspect). Suggestions section shows "No improvements needed" with a checkmark |
| 3 | Multiple checks fail in the same category | Category header shows red status dot with count (e.g., "3/8 passed"). Failed items sorted to the top within the expanded category for immediate visibility |
| 4 | Voice persona not configured | Voice Match signal shows 5.0/10 (baseline) with an info banner: "No voice persona configured. Set one up to get accurate voice matching." Links to `/settings/voice`. Score calculation uses 5.0 as the neutral default |
| 5 | Arabic content with different readability norms | Readability thresholds auto-adjust. The Readability signal bar shows an "AR" badge. Info tooltip explains: "Arabic Readability Index used instead of Flesch-Kincaid. Morphological analysis applied for vocabulary richness scoring." Passing threshold for readability shifts from 7.0 to 6.0 for Arabic |
| 6 | Revision loop exhausted (2 passes, still failing) | "Request Revision" button disabled. Banner: "Auto-revision limit reached. Manual editing required." Link to article editor. The best-scoring revision is marked as the "active" result |
| 7 | Score regresses after revision | Comparison view highlights regressions in red. Warning: "Revision 2 scored lower on Readability (6.8 -> 6.2). The system preserves the higher score as the official result." Official `overall_score` uses the maximum across all revisions |
| 8 | Very long article (10,000+ words) | Checklist items related to word count show the actual count. Scoring takes longer (estimated time shown). Some signals (AI Detection) may need chunked analysis — progress bar reflects chunk completion |
| 9 | Article has no images | Image-related checklist items (alt text, compression, lazy loading) are marked "N/A — no images in content" rather than "failed." These items are excluded from the pass/fail count and scoring |
| 10 | Concurrent scoring (two users trigger re-score simultaneously) | Server deduplicates: second request receives the in-progress scoring job ID. Both users see the same real-time progress. Optimistic locking prevents score overwrites |
| 11 | Browser offline during PDF export | PDF generation is fully client-side (all data already loaded). Export works offline. If data hasn't loaded yet, show "Report data not available offline" |
| 12 | Article deleted while viewing quality report | Next API poll returns 404. Show: "This article has been deleted. The quality report is no longer available." with link to article list. No crash or blank page |
| 13 | Checklist schema version mismatch (engine updated, old report) | Show banner: "This report was scored with an older checklist version (v2). Current version is v3. Re-score to get updated results." Existing data still displays correctly |
| 14 | Signal model returns score outside 0-10 range | Client-side clamping: `Math.max(0, Math.min(10, score))`. Log anomaly to error tracking. Display clamped value with no user-visible error |
| 15 | User navigates away during revision, returns later | Revision state persisted server-side. On return, page loads with revision-in-progress or completed state. No lost work |

**Edge case count: 15** (exceeds 10 minimum).

## 15. Open Questions & Dependencies

### Dependencies
- **Quality Gate Engine (Layer 4):** Must be operational for scoring. The 60-point checklist engine runs deterministic checks; the 7-signal rubric uses AI models for evaluation
- **Draft Writer (Layer 3):** Required for revision requests. Receives targeted fix instructions derived from the suggestions array
- **AI Detection Model:** External service dependency for the AI Detection signal. Must handle graceful degradation if unavailable
- **Voice Persona Store:** Voice Match signal requires a configured persona for the client. Falls back to 5.0 baseline if missing
- **Arabic NLP Pipeline:** Morphological analysis for Arabic readability scoring. Must be available when `articleLanguage === 'ar'`
- **Article Editor:** Linked from manual edit suggestions. Must support deep-linking to specific sections
- **PDF Generation Library:** Client-side library (e.g., `jspdf` + `html2canvas` or `@react-pdf/renderer`) for export functionality

### Open Questions
1. **Passing threshold:** Currently hardcoded at 7.0. Should this be configurable per workspace or per client? (Recommendation: workspace-level setting with 7.0 default, configurable in Settings)
2. **Score persistence:** When a revision scores lower, we preserve the higher score. Should we show both or only the official (higher) one? (Recommendation: show both with clear labeling of which is "official")
3. **Standalone mode access:** Should standalone scoring be available to all users or gated behind a plan tier? (Recommendation: available to all, rate-limited to 10/hour per workspace)
4. **Client-facing PDF branding:** Should the PDF include the agency's logo and branding, or ChainIQ's? (Recommendation: white-label — use the workspace's configured logo and brand colors)
5. **Checklist versioning:** When the checklist engine updates (e.g., from 60 to 65 items), should old reports auto-re-score or keep their original scores? (Recommendation: keep original, show "outdated version" banner with one-click re-score)
6. **Real-time scoring updates:** Current design uses polling (3s). Should we upgrade to WebSocket/SSE for scoring progress? (Recommendation: defer to Phase C — polling is sufficient for current scale)
7. **Bulk quality reports:** Should there be a way to view quality trends across multiple articles? (Recommendation: yes, but as a separate Dashboard widget, not on this screen. Link from here to the dashboard)
