# ChainIQ Pricing Strategy

> Pricing architecture, competitive benchmarking, growth tier proposal, discount structures, and pricing page copy framework for ChainIQ's 3-tier SaaS model targeting MENA publishers.

---

## Pricing Philosophy

ChainIQ's pricing is designed around three principles:

1. **Value-based, not cost-based** — Pricing reflects the value delivered (writer replacement, competitive advantage, revenue enablement), not the cost of compute
2. **Tier differentiation by capability, not just volume** — Each tier unlocks meaningfully different capabilities, not just more of the same
3. **Anchored to ROI** — Every tier's price is justifiable by a clear ROI calculation that the buyer can verify independently

### ROI Justification Per Tier

**Starter ($3,000/month) — Agency Justification:**
- Replaces 2-3 Arabic content writers ($2,400-$6,000/month in salaries)
- 100 articles/month at $30/article cost vs $150-$300/article for human writers
- Break-even: Bill 3-5 articles to clients at market rate ($500-$1,000 each)
- Net ROI: 150-400% in month 1

**Professional ($6,000/month) — Mid-Size Agency/Publisher Justification:**
- Replaces 5-8 writers ($6,000-$16,000/month)
- 500 articles/month across multiple clients or verticals
- Advanced voice profiles maintain distinct client brands
- API access enables custom workflow integration
- Net ROI: 200-500%

**Enterprise ($12,000/month) — Large Publisher Justification:**
- Unlimited content supports entire publishing operation
- Custom quality gates match existing editorial standards
- Dedicated support with contractual SLAs required by enterprise procurement
- Custom CMS integration eliminates workflow friction
- ROI: $12K/month replaces $50K-$100K/month in content production costs

## Growth Tier Proposal ($500-$800/month)

### Rationale
The current pricing creates a gap between solo creators ($0-$200/month budget) and agency buyers ($3,000+ budget). A Growth tier captures the mid-market: freelancers with multiple clients, small agencies (2-5 people), and individual enterprise content managers.

### Proposed Growth Tier

| Feature | Growth ($500-$800/mo) |
|---------|----------------------|
| **Articles/month** | 25 |
| **User seats** | 1 |
| **Languages** | Arabic + English |
| **Voice profiles** | 1 |
| **Content briefs** | Included |
| **Keyword intelligence** | Basic |
| **SERP analysis** | Google AE only |
| **Quality gates** | Standard |
| **API access** | No |
| **CMS integration** | WordPress only |
| **Support** | Email (48h response) |
| **Onboarding** | Self-serve |

### Growth Tier Economics
- **Target price point:** $650/month (tested via price sensitivity survey)
- **Target volume:** 50-100 Growth customers by Month 12
- **Revenue contribution:** $32,500-$65,000/month (supplements enterprise revenue)
- **Conversion goal:** 20% of Growth customers upgrade to Starter within 6 months
- **Churn expectation:** Higher than Starter (10-15% monthly) — acceptable for self-serve tier

### Launch Timing
- **Not at launch** — Growth tier launches after Starter/Professional/Enterprise tiers are validated with paying customers
- **Target launch:** Month 4-6, after initial customer feedback confirms feature set priorities
- **Risk:** Growth tier could cannibalize Starter tier. Mitigation: clear capability gaps between Growth (basic) and Starter (advanced)

## Discount Structures

### Annual Discount
- **Discount:** 20% off monthly price when paid annually
- **Annual pricing:** Starter $28,800/year ($2,400/mo effective), Professional $57,600/year ($4,800/mo effective), Enterprise $115,200/year ($9,600/mo effective)
- **Cash flow benefit:** Full year payment upfront improves cash position
- **Churn benefit:** Annual contracts reduce monthly churn decision points
- **Target:** 40% of customers on annual plans by Month 6

### Enterprise Custom Pricing
- For organizations requiring:
  - Custom SLAs (99.99% uptime guarantee)
  - Data residency requirements (GCC-hosted infrastructure)
  - Custom integrations beyond standard offerings
  - Volume exceeding Enterprise tier standard allocations
  - Multi-division or multi-publication deployments
- **Floor price:** $15,000/month ($180,000/year)
- **Ceiling:** Determined per engagement, typically $25,000-$50,000/month for large publishers
- **Procurement:** Enterprise deals go through custom proposal and negotiation process

### Promotional Discounts (Launch Phase)
- **Founding customer discount:** First 10 customers receive 30% off for the first 12 months (locks in early adopters, creates case study opportunities)
- **Agency partner discount:** Agencies referring 3+ clients receive 15% ongoing discount on their own subscription
- **Annual commitment bonus:** Sign annual contract in first 30 days, receive onboarding program ($5,000 value) free

## Pricing Review Cadence

| Review | Frequency | Triggers for Change |
|--------|-----------|-------------------|
| Competitive pricing scan | Quarterly | Competitor raises/lowers prices by > 20% |
| Customer feedback on pricing | Monthly | > 20% of prospects cite price as primary objection |
| Tier feature allocation | Quarterly | Feature usage data suggests tier boundaries are wrong |
| Growth tier validation | Month 4-6 | Sufficient data from early customers to validate mid-market demand |
| Enterprise pricing review | Per-deal | Each enterprise deal informs future custom pricing |

### Pricing Principles for Changes
- Never reduce prices for existing customers (grandfather existing rates)
- Price increases: 90-day advance notice for existing customers, applied at renewal
- New features added to existing tiers do not trigger price changes
- Feature removal from tiers requires migration path and 6-month notice
