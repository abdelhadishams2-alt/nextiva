# Persona 03: Shopify Store Owner

## Sarah Bergmann -- Founder and CEO, Glow Theory (DTC Beauty Brand on Shopify)

---

## 1. Who I Am

I am Sarah Bergmann, founder and CEO of Glow Theory, a direct-to-consumer clean beauty brand selling skincare products through Shopify. We did $2M in revenue last year and are targeting $3.5M this year. My team is small: myself, a part-time operations manager, a customer service rep, a social media coordinator, and a freelance content writer I hire on a per-article basis.

I have a business background, not a technical one. I can manage our Shopify store, run Facebook and Google Ads (poorly -- I outsource most of it), and I understand the basics of SEO from reading blogs and taking an online course two years ago. I know organic traffic matters. I know content marketing can reduce my customer acquisition cost. But I do not know how to build a content strategy, and honestly, I do not have time to learn. I am running a business -- I wear 12 hats and content marketing is hat number 9.

My blog exists because everyone told me I needed one. It has about 40 posts, most written by the freelancer I hired 8 months ago. The articles get some traffic but almost none of it converts. I am paying $200-400 per article for content that ranks for random keywords but does not drive product sales. I feel like I am burning money on content, but I also know that my competitors who have strong blogs are growing faster than I am.

---

## 2. My Daily Workflow

**7:00 AM** -- I check Shopify analytics: yesterday revenue, conversion rate, top products, and traffic sources. Organic search is our third-largest channel (behind paid ads and email), but it is growing slowly. I see the traffic, but I have no idea which blog posts are driving it or whether any of that traffic converts to sales.

**7:30 AM** -- I check email and Slack. If my freelancer has submitted a draft, I review it. My review process is: Does it sound like our brand? Does it mention our products naturally? Is it not embarrassingly bad? That is the extent of my editorial process. I do not have an SEO checklist, a style guide, or a content calendar. I know I should, but I do not have time to create them.

**8:00-11:00 AM** -- Core business operations: inventory management, supplier communications, shipping issues, customer escalations. Content is not on my radar during these hours.

**11:00 AM** -- I might spend 30 minutes trying to come up with blog post ideas. My process: I look at what competitors are writing about, I check Google autocomplete for ideas related to our products, and I ask my social media coordinator what questions customers are asking on Instagram. I usually come up with 1-2 vague ideas ("maybe something about retinol?") and send them to the freelancer with minimal direction.

**12:00-4:00 PM** -- More operations, ad management review, meetings with suppliers or potential retail partners.

**4:00 PM** -- If I have time, I will look at Google Search Console (which I check maybe once a week). I see which pages get impressions and clicks, but I do not know how to turn that data into action. I see that a blog post about "niacinamide benefits" gets 2,000 impressions a month but only 30 clicks. I do not know what to do with that information.

**Evening** -- I sometimes browse competitor blogs and feel a mix of admiration and frustration. They have beautiful, comprehensive content that clearly drives traffic. I do not have the team, knowledge, or budget to replicate that.

---

## 3. My Top 5 Pain Points

1. **I do not know what to write.** This is my fundamental problem. I have 35 products. I know my customers ask questions about ingredients, routines, skin types, and comparisons. But I have no systematic way to identify which topics will actually drive organic traffic to my product pages. I am guessing, and my guesses are not data-driven. My freelancer does not know either -- she writes whatever I tell her to, and I am telling her the wrong things.

2. **My blog content does not convert.** I have 40 blog posts. According to GA4, they get a few thousand visits per month in aggregate. But when I look at the conversion data, almost none of that blog traffic leads to product page views or purchases. The content exists in isolation -- it ranks for informational keywords but has no commercial intent and no product integration. It is traffic for the sake of traffic.

3. **My freelancer produces generic SEO content.** I do not blame her -- I give her vague briefs and she does her best. But the articles she produces could be on any beauty blog. They do not mention our products, they do not link to our product pages strategically, and they do not reflect our brand voice (clean, science-forward, slightly irreverent). They read like they were written by someone who has never used our products -- because they have not.

4. **I cannot afford an SEO agency, and I cannot do it myself.** SEO agencies want $3-5K/month minimum. I cannot justify that when my total content budget is $2K/month. But doing SEO myself means learning a discipline that takes years to master, and I do not have the time. I am stuck in a middle ground where I know content marketing matters but I cannot execute it effectively.

5. **I have no product-to-content mapping.** I have 35 SKUs, each with specific use cases, ingredient stories, and customer questions. But there is no connection between my product catalog and my content strategy. My blog lives in a completely separate world from my product pages. I need content that bridges the two -- that attracts organic traffic for product-relevant queries and naturally guides readers toward my products.

---

## 4. My Deal-Breakers

- **Shopify integration that actually works.** Not "you can copy-paste HTML into Shopify." I mean native Shopify integration that understands my product catalog, my collections, my blog infrastructure, and can publish directly. If I have to do manual work to get content from ChainIQ to my Shopify store, the friction will kill my adoption.

- **Product-aware content intelligence.** The system must understand my product catalog and generate content recommendations that connect informational search queries to my products. "Write about retinol" is useless. "Write about retinol for sensitive skin, linking to your Gentle Retinol Serum" is what I need.

- **Simplicity.** I am not a power user. I do not want 47 dashboard tabs. I want: what should I write, here is the draft, publish it. If the learning curve is more than an hour, I will not use it. If it requires me to understand SEO jargon (topical authority, content silos, TF-IDF), I will bounce.

- **Price under $500/month.** I am a $2M business spending $2K/month on content. If ChainIQ costs $3K/month, the economics do not work for me. I need a pricing tier that makes sense for small e-commerce businesses. Even $500/month would need to demonstrably outperform my current $200/article freelancer approach.

- **Content that sounds like my brand, not like a generic SEO blog.** If the output reads like every other beauty blog on the internet -- bland, keyword-stuffed, personality-free -- it is worse than what my freelancer produces because at least she tries to match my tone.

## 6. My Wish List

1. **Given** I have connected my Shopify store and GSC account, **When** I ask ChainIQ "what should I write?", **Then** I expect a list of 10 blog post recommendations that are specifically tied to my product catalog -- each recommendation showing which product it supports, the target keyword, estimated search volume, and a one-sentence angle -- presented in plain language, not SEO jargon.

2. **Given** I have selected a topic from the recommendations, **When** I click "Generate Article," **Then** I expect a near-publication-ready blog post (1,200-1,800 words) that naturally mentions 1-3 of my products with genuine purchase-intent context, includes internal links to the relevant product pages, and matches my brand voice -- all within 10 minutes.

3. **Given** I have approved an article, **When** I click "Publish to Shopify," **Then** I expect the article to appear on my Shopify blog with correct formatting, product links, featured image suggestions, meta title, meta description, and proper URL slug -- without me touching the Shopify blog editor.

4. **Given** I published an article 60 days ago, **When** I check its performance, **Then** I expect to see not just traffic numbers but product attribution: "This article drove 147 visits to your Gentle Retinol Serum product page and contributed to an estimated 12 purchases" -- connecting content directly to revenue.

5. **Given** a customer frequently asks on Instagram "what is the difference between your Vitamin C serum and the retinol serum?", **When** I input that question into ChainIQ, **Then** I expect it to generate a comparison blog post that is both SEO-optimized for relevant keywords and genuinely useful for my customers -- not a thin keyword-targeted article but a real product comparison that drives informed purchases.

---

## 7. Competitor Envy

I am envious of what Yotpo does for review-driven content and what Klaviyo does for email-driven content -- both tools understand my product catalog and generate content that is product-aware. No SEO content tool does this. Every blog content tool I have tried treats my blog as separate from my store. They do not know what I sell, what my best-selling products are, or what questions my customers ask about them.

I also look at brands like Glossier and The Ordinary and envy their content marketing. Their blogs are not just SEO content -- they are product education that happens to rank well. The content is genuinely useful, product-integrated, and brand-consistent. I want that, but I do not have a content team of 10 to produce it.

Jasper.ai has e-commerce templates, but they produce generic output that does not understand my specific products. The "product description" template does not know that my Vitamin C serum uses a stabilized form of ascorbic acid, or that my customers care about that distinction.

---

## 8. If I Could Change ONE Thing

Make my product catalog the starting point for content strategy, not an afterthought. Every content tool starts with keywords. I need a tool that starts with my products -- their ingredients, their use cases, their customer questions, their competitive positioning -- and then reverse-engineers the content strategy that will drive organic traffic to those products. The keyword research should serve the product strategy, not the other way around.

---

## 9. What Would Make Me Evangelize

If I could point to ChainIQ and say, "This tool increased my organic traffic by 300% in 6 months and that traffic converts at 2x my paid traffic," I would tell every Shopify store owner I know. The DTC e-commerce community is extremely active on Twitter, in Slack groups, and in communities like Shopify forums. We all have the same problem: we know content marketing works, but we cannot execute it at the level of the big brands.

If ChainIQ made me feel like I had a content marketing team even though I am a solo founder, and if the content it produced actually drove product sales (not just vanity traffic), I would write a case study, post it on Twitter, share it in every DTC community I am in, and recommend it on every podcast that will have me. Small e-commerce founders talk to each other constantly, and a tool that genuinely solves the "what do I write and will it sell my products" problem would spread virally.

---

## 10. Red Flags

- **Enterprise-first design.** If the onboarding process assumes I have an SEO team, a CMS administrator, and API credentials ready to go, it was not designed for someone like me. If the pricing page starts at $3K/month with no small-business tier, I am not the target customer and I will not try to make it work.

- **SEO jargon overload.** If the interface is full of terms like "topical authority score," "content velocity," "SERP feature capture rate," and "entity salience" without plain-language explanations, I will feel like it was built for SEO professionals, not business owners. I will feel stupid, and I will close the tab.

- **Content that ignores my products.** If the generated content is beautifully optimized for keywords but never mentions my actual products, or mentions them in an awkward shoe-horned way, it fails at the one job I need it to do.

- **Complex setup requirements.** If getting started requires connecting 5 different tools, configuring API keys, installing plugins, and going through a 30-minute onboarding wizard, I will abandon it before I finish. I need: connect Shopify, connect Google, see recommendations. Three steps.

- **No revenue attribution.** If the performance tracking only shows me traffic and rankings but not product page views and revenue impact, it is not measuring what matters to me. I do not run a media company. I run a store. Traffic that does not convert is a cost, not a benefit.

- **Freelancer replacement messaging.** If ChainIQ positions itself as "fire your freelancer," I will be suspicious. I want a tool that makes my freelancer more effective, or that produces content good enough that I can reduce my freelancer dependency gradually -- but positioning against human writers feels dishonest.
