# Persona 09: Rachel Torres — Head of Marketing, E-Commerce (Outdoor Gear)

## Who I Am

I'm Rachel Torres, 34, Head of Marketing at Summit Trail Co., a direct-to-consumer outdoor gear brand doing roughly $5M/year in revenue on Shopify Plus. We sell everything from ultralight tents to hiking boots to technical apparel. I report to the CEO/founder and manage a lean team of two content people — one writer who came from outdoor journalism, and one junior marketer who handles social media and email but also pitches in on blog posts.

Content is the single most important non-paid channel we have. Our blog drives 35% of our organic revenue — roughly $1.75M/year flows through articles like "Best Ultralight Backpacking Tents for Thru-Hiking" and "How to Layer for Winter Camping." These aren't throwaway SEO bait; they're genuinely useful guides that convert because they reference our actual products with honest comparisons against competitors. The problem is we produce 8 blog posts per month and I know we should be doing 25-30 to capture the long tail of outdoor gear queries we're leaving on the table.

Before this role I spent five years at a mid-size DTC brand where I learned the hard way that generic content doesn't convert in e-commerce. Content has to be product-aware — it needs to know our catalog, our price points, what's in stock, what's seasonal. If an article recommends a tent we discontinued last quarter, that's worse than no article at all.

I'm technical enough to manage our Shopify theme, set up basic Liquid snippets, and read GA4 reports. I'm not a developer. I evaluate tools based on whether they save my team hours without sacrificing the quality bar that built our brand trust.

## Daily Workflow

My day starts with checking GA4 and Shopify analytics — what content drove sales yesterday, which product pages are getting organic traffic, are there any conversion rate drops I need to investigate. Then I review our editorial calendar in Notion, check in with my writer on drafts in progress, and usually spend an hour on keyword research in Ahrefs trying to find gaps where we could rank.

Twice a week I manually pull data from Google Search Console to see which queries we're showing up for but not ranking well on — those become content briefs. Writing those briefs takes me 45-60 minutes each because I have to cross-reference our product catalog, check what competitors like REI and Backcountry have published, and figure out the seasonal angle.

Publishing is painful. My writer drafts in Google Docs, I edit, then either I or the junior marketer manually copy the HTML into Shopify's blog editor, fix formatting issues, add product links, upload images, set meta tags. This copy-paste-fix cycle takes 30-45 minutes per article and introduces errors constantly — broken links, missing alt tags, wrong product URLs.

On Fridays I do a content performance review: which articles are ranking, which ones need updating, and what new opportunities Ahrefs is surfacing. I also spend time analyzing competitor content from REI, Switchback Travel, and Outdoor Gear Lab to understand what's working in the space.

## Top 5 Pain Points

### 1. We Can't Scale Content Production Without Hiring
At 8 posts/month we're capturing maybe 15% of the keyword opportunities I've identified. I've mapped out 200+ article topics that could drive revenue. But my writer can only produce 2 quality posts per week, and hiring a second experienced outdoor writer would cost $65-80K/year. I need a way to produce more content at the same quality bar without doubling headcount.

### 2. Content Briefs Are Manual and Time-Consuming
Every brief I write requires cross-referencing 4-5 tools: Ahrefs for keywords, GSC for existing performance, our Shopify catalog for relevant products, competitor sites for content gaps, and seasonal sales data for timing. There's no single tool that synthesizes all of this. I spend 6-8 hours per week just on brief creation.

### 3. Product Catalog Awareness Is Missing from Every Content Tool
No AI writing tool I've tried knows about our product catalog. They'll write generic "best hiking boots" content that doesn't mention our actual products, prices, or unique selling points. I end up spending as much time injecting product references as I would writing from scratch. I need a tool that can pull from our Shopify catalog and intelligently weave products into content.

### 4. Publishing to Shopify Blog Is a Manual HTML Nightmare
Shopify's blog editor is basic. Every article requires manual HTML cleanup, product link insertion, image uploading, and meta tag configuration. My team loses 4-6 hours per week on publishing tasks that should be automated. We've looked at headless CMS solutions but the migration cost is prohibitive.

### 5. Seasonal Content Timing Is Guesswork
Outdoor gear buying is intensely seasonal — tent content needs to peak in March-April, winter layering content in September-October. But I'm making timing decisions based on intuition and last year's GA4 data. I want a system that tells me "publish this tent comparison by March 15 to catch the buying cycle" based on actual search trend data.

## Deal-Breakers

- **No Shopify integration = no deal.** If I have to copy-paste HTML into Shopify, the tool hasn't solved my biggest pain point. I need direct publishing to Shopify blog with proper formatting, product links, and meta tags.
- **No product catalog awareness = no deal.** If the tool can't reference our actual products — names, prices, URLs, availability — the content it produces is useless to me. Generic content doesn't convert in e-commerce.
- **Content quality below our brand standard.** Our readers are experienced outdoor enthusiasts. If the AI produces generic, surface-level content that reads like it was written by someone who's never been on a trail, it will damage our brand. Voice matching is critical.

## Wish List (5 User Stories)

1. **As a marketing head**, I want ChainIQ to ingest my Shopify product catalog so that generated content automatically references real products with correct names, prices, and links — eliminating manual product insertion.

2. **As a content strategist**, I want a seasonal content calendar that tells me which topics to publish and when, based on historical search trends and our sales data, so I can plan content 2-3 months ahead of buying cycles.

3. **As a Shopify store operator**, I want to publish finished articles directly to our Shopify blog with proper HTML formatting, internal product links, meta descriptions, and featured images — no copy-paste required.

4. **As a brand steward**, I want the AI to learn my writer's voice from our existing 150+ blog posts so that AI-assisted content is indistinguishable from what she would write, maintaining the authentic tone our readers trust.

5. **As a revenue-focused marketer**, I want to see which AI-generated articles are driving actual product sales (not just pageviews or rankings) so I can optimize our content strategy around revenue impact.

## Competitor Envy

**Jasper** has decent templates and brand voice features, but it knows nothing about my products or Shopify. I tried it for three months and every article needed 60-90 minutes of editing to add product context. Not worth it.

**Surfer SEO** is great for on-page optimization but it's a writing assistant, not a content strategy engine. It tells me how to optimize a page I've already decided to write, not what I should be writing next.

**REI's content operation** is what I envy most. They have a massive content team producing product-aware guides that rank for everything. I want the output quality of a 20-person editorial team with my 2-person crew.

**Shopify's native AI tools** are too basic — they can generate product descriptions but can't produce the long-form, comparison-style content that drives our organic revenue.

## If I Could Change ONE Thing

Give me a "content intelligence engine" that connects to my Shopify catalog, my GA4 data, and my Ahrefs account, and tells me every Monday morning: "Here are the 5 articles you should publish this week, here are the products to feature in each, and here's why these topics will drive revenue based on seasonal trends and search demand." That single feature would transform my content operation from reactive to proactive.

## What Would Make Me Evangelize

If ChainIQ helped my 2-person team produce 20+ high-quality, product-aware articles per month that drove measurable revenue growth — and published them directly to Shopify without manual intervention — I would talk about it at every e-commerce event I attend. I'd write a case study. I'd refer every DTC brand I know.

The bar is: show me a 2x increase in content output with no decrease in quality or conversion rate, and save my team 10+ hours per week on publishing and brief creation. Hit those numbers and I'm your loudest advocate.

## Red Flags

- **"Works with Shopify" that actually means "exports HTML you paste into Shopify."** I've been burned by this before. If the integration isn't native — creating blog posts via the Shopify API with proper metafields — it's not a real integration.
- **Generic content that ignores my catalog.** If I test the tool and the first article mentions products we don't sell, I'll cancel immediately.
- **No revenue tracking.** If performance tracking only shows rankings and traffic but can't connect to Shopify sales data, it's missing the metric that matters most to me.
- **Onboarding that requires a developer.** I don't have a dev on my team. If setup requires custom API work or Liquid template modifications, I need that handled during onboarding.
- **Annual contracts with no pilot period.** At $3-5K/month, I need to prove ROI before committing long-term. Give me 60-90 days to show results or I can't justify the spend to my CEO.
